# E-Commerce Cohort Analysis

## Project Overview

This project was completed as part of the TripleTen Business Intelligence Analytics program and focused on transforming raw user event logs into actionable business insights for an e-commerce company. The objective was to analyze customer behavior across the website, measure conversion performance, and evaluate customer retention over time using cohort analysis. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

## Business Problem

The company needed to better understand how users moved through the sales funnel—from product views to completed purchases—and how well newly acquired customers were retained after their first purchase. Leadership wanted data-driven insights to improve conversion rates, customer engagement, and long-term revenue growth. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

## Key Analyses Performed

### Conversion Funnel

Built a three-stage conversion funnel using unique user counts to measure progression from:

1. Product Page Views  
2. Shopping Cart Opens  
3. Completed Purchases  

Calculated:

- Total conversion rates  
- Step-to-step conversion rates :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

### Cohort Retention Analysis

Created customer acquisition cohorts based on the month of each user's first purchase and tracked retention performance monthly.

Calculated:

- First purchase month  
- Cohort age (0–4 months)  
- Monthly returning customer percentages  
- Retention trends across six customer cohorts :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

## Key Insights

- Identified drop-off points in the purchase funnel where customers failed to convert  
- Measured how effectively the website turned visitors into buyers  
- Revealed customer retention patterns after first purchase  
- Compared retention strength between acquisition cohorts  
- Provided actionable recommendations for improving repeat purchases and conversion efficiency
